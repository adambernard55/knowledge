# Template: Semantic Depth Content Brief

## Instructions

This brief must be completed before any new content is written. Its purpose is to ensure every article is strategically designed to achieve Semantic Depth, as defined in the [[kb/SEO/8_frameworks-and-sops/01_semantic-depth-standard|Semantic Depth Standard]].

---

> ## **Content Brief: [Article Title]**
> 
> ---
> 
> ### **1. Core Strategic Details**
> 
> -   **Primary Keyword:** `[Insert primary search term]`
> -   **Target Audience:** `[Describe the ideal reader and their level of expertise]`
> -   **Primary User Goal:** `[Complete this sentence: After reading this, the user will be able to...]`
> 
> ---
> 
> ### **2. Conceptual Mapping (System-Aligned Meaning)**
> 
> -   **Primary Concept:** `[What is the core idea, entity, or topic? e.g., "Roth IRA"]`
> -   **Related Concepts & Entities:** `[List related nouns and ideas that must be mentioned, e.g., "401(k)", "Contribution Limits", "Tax-advantaged growth", "IRS"]`
> -   **Common Misconceptions to Address:** `[What do people usually get wrong about this topic?]`
> -   **Key User Questions to Answer:**
>     -   `[Question 1, e.g., "What are the income limits for a Roth IRA?"]`
>     -   `[Question 2, e.g., "Can I have a Roth IRA and a 401(k)?"]`
>     -   `[Add more as needed]`
> 
> ---
> 
> ### **3. Content Quality & Originality (People-First Completeness)**
> 
> -   **Originality Angle:** `[How will this content be unique? e.g., "Includes a custom-designed comparison chart," "Features quotes from three financial experts," "Based on our firsthand experience using the product."]`
> -   **Key Sources to Cite:** `[List authoritative external sources to reference, e.g., IRS.gov, Investopedia]`
> -   **Internal Linking Opportunities:** `[List 2-3 existing articles to link to]`
> 
> ---
> 
> ### **4. Pre-Submission QA Checklist**
> 
> -   [ ] **Completeness:** Does this content provide a substantial, complete, or comprehensive description of the topic?
> -   [ ] **Goal Completion:** Will the reader feel they have learned enough to achieve their primary goal?
> -   [ ] **Originality:** Does this content provide original information, reporting, or analysis not found elsewhere?
> -   [ ] **Credibility:** Is the content clearly sourced with evidence of the expertise involved?
